### Key Components
- **SORM Reports**: The archive houses the complete public release of the Structural Observation Reverse-Mapping (SORM) report series.

### Content & Use

---

### What "a single cognitive subject" means

In the context of scientific and structural research, "a single cognitive subject" means that all the data, mapping, and reports in the archive focus entirely on one individual mind or system. Instead of studying a broad group of people or multiple different AI models to find general trends, this research is a single-case study. Every framework tested in the Negentropic Kernel Archive is applied to this same, identical subject.

Why focus on a single subject?
- **Deep Architecture Mapping**: Studying one subject allows researchers to create a highly detailed, comprehensive map of its specific cognitive structure.
- **Controlled Cross-Validation**: Testing the exact same subject across six distinct theoretical frameworks ensures variations come from differences in analytical methods, not subject differences.
- **Isolating Variables**: A single-subject focus eliminates external variables.

Think of it like analyzing the structural blueprints of one specific building from six different engineering perspectives.

---

### The six independent theoretical frameworks

1. **The OODA Loop (John Boyd)** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act. Measures how the subject updates actions when faced with new data.
2. **The SRK Framework (Jens Rasmussen)** — Skill-based, Rule-based, Knowledge-based tiers.
3. **Situation Awareness Model (Mica Endsley)** — Perception, Comprehension, Projection.
4. **Game Theory (John Harsanyi & John Nash)** — Strategic logic and Nash Equilibria.
5. **Complex Adaptive Systems (John Holland & Stuart Kauffman)** — System as living, fluid network.
6. **Information Topology / Cybernetic Coherence** — Geometric mapping of structural integrity.

### Anonymity of the subject

The Negentropic Kernel Archive keeps the identity of the single cognitive subject entirely anonymous and un-named. Instead, the documentation focuses on the system's structural data, behaviors, and logic patterns.

As noted in the Archive's Methodology Documentation: "The analytical tool does not define the subject; the subject selects the framework."

In short, the subject is a blind baseline study — an unidentified mind or cognitive network being run through a gauntlet of structural stress tests.

### What a SORM report is

A SORM (Structural Observation Reverse-Mapping) report tracks how a cognitive system maps information. As stated in the Archive's Methodology Documentation: "The structure existed before the language to describe it. The language is translation."

A typical SORM report follows a modular breakdown: Input Disruption Capture → State Mapping → Reverse-Mapping Analysis → Cross-Framework Integration → Coherence & Entropy Scoring.

The goal is translation without alteration.
